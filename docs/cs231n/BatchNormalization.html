Batch Normalization¶

One way to make deep networks easier to train is to use more sophisticated optimization procedures such as SGD+momentum, RMSProp, or Adam. Another strategy is to change the architecture of the network to make it easier to train. One idea along these lines is batch normalization which was proposed by [3] in 2015.

The idea is relatively straightforward. Machine learning methods tend to work better when their input data consists of uncorrelated features with zero mean and unit variance. When training a neural network, we can preprocess the data before feeding it to the network to explicitly decorrelate its features; this will ensure that the first layer of the network sees data that follows a nice distribution. However, even if we preprocess the input data, the activations at deeper layers of the network will likely no longer be decorrelated and will no longer have zero mean or unit variance since they are output from earlier layers in the network. Even worse, during the training process the distribution of features at each layer of the network will shift as the weights of each layer are updated.

The authors of [3] hypothesize that the shifting distribution of features inside deep neural networks may make training deep networks more difficult. To overcome this problem, [3] proposes to insert batch normalization layers into the network. At training time, a batch normalization layer uses a minibatch of data to estimate the mean and standard deviation of each feature. These estimated means and standard deviations are then used to center and normalize the features of the minibatch. A running average of these means and standard deviations is kept during training, and at test time these running averages are used to center and normalize features.

It is possible that this normalization strategy could reduce the representational power of the network, since it may sometimes be optimal for certain layers to have features that are not zero-mean or unit variance. To this end, the batch normalization layer includes learnable shift and scale parameters for each feature dimension.

[3] Sergey Ioffe and Christian Szegedy, "Batch Normalization: Accelerating Deep Network Training by Reducing Internal Covariate Shift", ICML 2015.

batch normalization

这里要求实现batch normalization（BN）的正向传播。BN层处于全连接层之后，激活函数层(如ReLU)层之前。
使用BN的目的是把全连接层之后把数据强行执行中心化(均值为0)和标准化(方差为1)，避免某个维度上的数值太大或太小，导致权重训练不能平稳进行，从而提高网络的效果。
batch normalization的过程就是对于到来的一个batch的数据，同一维度但不同数据点的数值进行减去均值再除以方差的方法进行归一化。对于一个batch的数据，第n个数据点的第d个维度的数据归一化后的值为：
$$ \hat{x_{nd}} = \frac{x_{nd}-\mu_d}{\sqrt{\sigma^2_d+\epsilon}} $$ 其中$\mu_d$是d维度的不同数据点之间求出的均值，$\sigma^2_d$则是对应的方差。

计算总体均值方差

注意到上面陈述的过程中，不同的batch的数据，即使同个维度他们用来归一化的均值和方差也是不同的。那么对于测试集的数据，归一化使用的均值和方差如何得到呢。
这里采用的方法，是对batch之间的均值和方差做一个滑动平均，和动量法一样，使用最后得到的滑动平均值作为测试集使用的均值和方差。这个方法有个问题，就是数据的顺序会影响最后的均值和方差，后面的batch数据对测试集使用的均值方差有较大的影响。
$$ \mu_{test_t} = \rho_1 \mu_{test_{t-1}} + (1-\rho_1) \mu_{train_t}\\ \sigma^2_{test_t} = \rho_2 \sigma^2_{test_{t-1}} + (1-\rho_2) \sigma^2_{train_t} $$ $\mu_{test_i}，\sigma^2_{test_i}$是指第i次迭代得到的可以用于测试集的均值和方差。
$\mu_{train_i}，\sigma^2_{train_i}$是指第i次迭代训练集计算出来的均值和方差。
$\rho$是一个自定义的小于1的比例。
更好的方法是把所有的均值和方法求平均值，但是这个需要较大的空间保存所有的均值和方差。这里是一个需要权衡的点，实验中不采用。

偏移归一化后的数据

BN层最后还对一个同一维度的数据乘上一个参数$\gamma$并加上另一个参数$\beta$，第n个数据点的第d个维度的数据最后的输出为：
$$ y_{nd} = \gamma_d \hat{x_{nd}} + \beta_d $$ $gamma$和$beta$两个参数是随网络的训练进行更新，希望把数据的中心和方差调节到适合的点。

BN层的反向传播

这里给的输入有上层的求导结果，也就是对BN层输出y的求导结果$\frac{dL}{dy}$。
cache中有
正向传播后传过来的BN层输入x
需要训练的参数$\beta$和$\gamma$
正向传播过程得到的batch的均值$\mu_B$和方差$\sigma_B$。
正向传播过程得到的归一化的数据$\hat{x}$
需要求的是三个导数：$\frac{dL}{dx}, \frac{dL}{d\gamma}, \frac{dL}{d\beta}$

对输入x的求导

根据链式法则有： $$ \frac{dL}{dx} = \frac{dL}{d\hat{x}}*\frac{d\hat{x}}{x} + \frac{dL}{d\sigma^2}*\frac{d\sigma^2}{x} + \frac{dL}{d\mu}*\frac{d\mu}{dx} $$ 接下来推导每一项的计算： $$ \begin{align} \frac{dL}{d\hat{x}} &= \frac{dL}{dy} * \frac{dy}{d\hat{x}} = \frac{dL}{dy} * \gamma\\ \frac{d\hat{x}}{dx} &= \frac{dL}{dy} * \frac{1}{\sqrt{\sigma^2_d+\epsilon}}\\ \frac{dL}{d\sigma^2} &= \sum{\frac{\frac{dL}{d\hat{x}} * [-(x-\mu)]}{2(\sigma^2+\epsilon)^{-\frac{3}{2}}}} \\ \frac{d\sigma^2}{x} &= \frac{2(x-\mu)}{m}\\ \frac{dL}{d\mu} &= (\sum \frac{dL}{d\hat{x}}\frac{-1}{\sqrt{\sigma^2_d+\epsilon}}) + \frac{dL}{d\sigma^2}\frac{\sum -2(x-\mu)}{m}\\ \frac{d\mu}{dx} &= \frac{1}{m} \end{align} $$ 上面的计算在实现的时候需要注意每个计算量的形状。公式里的每个变量可能是向量，矩阵，标量，需要弄清楚。
对于$\frac{dx}{dy}$形式的变量，其形状和$y$的形状一致。
于是$\frac{dL}{d\sigma^2}$ 的形状应该是$\sigma^2$的形状，也就是D维的向量，其中D为输入维度大小。等号右边的$\sum$后面却是形状为N×D的矩阵，其中N是batch size，可见求和应该是沿着N的维度求和得到D维向量。
同样$\frac{dL}{d\mu}$也是D维的向量，第一个$\sum$后面也是N×D维的矩阵，应该沿着N维求和。第二个$\sum$后面的矩阵由于是由$x$计算出来，也应该是N×D的矩阵，求和结果其实是x的总数减去x均值的总数，于是后面一项为0。

参数的求导 ($\gamma \& \beta$)

$$ \frac{dL}{d\gamma} = \sum\frac{dL}{dy}*\hat{x}\\ \frac{dL}{d\beta} = \sum\frac{dL}{dy} $$ $\gamma$和$\beta$都是D维的向量，于是求和也是沿着N维的方向进行求和。 </font>

Batch normalization: alternative backward¶

In class we talked about two different implementations for the sigmoid backward pass. One strategy is to write out a computation graph composed of simple operations and backprop through all intermediate values. Another strategy is to work out the derivatives on paper. For example, you can derive a very simple formula for the sigmoid function's backward pass by simplifying gradients on paper.

Surprisingly, it turns out that you can do a similar simplification for the batch normalization backward pass too.
Given a set of inputs $X=\begin{bmatrix}x_1\\x_2\\...\\x_N\end{bmatrix}$, we first calculate the mean $\mu=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=1}^N x_k$ and variance $v=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=1}^N (x_k-\mu)^2.$
With $\mu$ and $v$ calculated, we can calculate the standard deviation $\sigma=\sqrt{v+\epsilon}$ and normalized data $Y$ with $y_i=\frac{x_i-\mu}{\sigma}.$

The meat of our problem is to get $\frac{\partial L}{\partial X}$ from the upstream gradient $\frac{\partial L}{\partial Y}.$ It might be challenging to directly reason about the gradients over $X$ and $Y$ - try reasoning about it in terms of $x_i$ and $y_i$ first.

You will need to come up with the derivations for $\frac{\partial L}{\partial x_i}$, by relying on the Chain Rule to first calculate the intermediate $\frac{\partial \mu}{\partial x_i}, \frac{\partial v}{\partial x_i}, \frac{\partial \sigma}{\partial x_i},$ then assemble these pieces to calculate $\frac{\partial y_i}{\partial x_i}$. You should make sure each of the intermediary steps are all as simple as possible.

After doing so, implement the simplified batch normalization backward pass in the function batchnorm_backward_alt and compare the two implementations by running the following. Your two implementations should compute nearly identical results, but the alternative implementation should be a bit faster.

Layer Normalization¶

Batch normalization has proved to be effective in making networks easier to train, but the dependency on batch size makes it less useful in complex networks which have a cap on the input batch size due to hardware limitations.

Several alternatives to batch normalization have been proposed to mitigate(减轻) this problem; one such technique is Layer Normalization [4]. Instead of normalizing over the batch, we normalize over the features. In other words, when using Layer Normalization, each feature vector corresponding to a single datapoint is normalized based on the sum of all terms within that feature vector.

[4] Ba, Jimmy Lei, Jamie Ryan Kiros, and Geoffrey E. Hinton. "Layer Normalization." stat 1050 (2016): 21.

One significant difference though is that for layer normalization, we do not keep track of the moving moments, and the testing phase is identical to the training phase, where the mean and variance are directly calculated per datapoint.

Layer normalization

沿着batch normalization的思路，batch normalization的效果随着batch size而发生变化。然而有的时候，batch size无法无限制增大，这就限制了batch normalization的能力。于是又提出layer normalization，和batch normalization不同的是，layer normalization沿着一个数据点的方向进行归一化。对于到来的输入数据，我们针对的是一个数据点，减去其各个维度数值的均值，除以对应方差。
这样一来，再也不用考虑测试数据需要用什么均值什么方差来归一化了，因为均值和方差都是根据当前数据点计算出来的。同时这些均值和方差和batch size没有关系，归一化效果不受batch size制约。 $$ \hat{x} = \frac{x-\mu}{\sqrt{\sigma^2+\epsilon}} $$ $\hat{x}$表示任意的数据点，$\mu$和$\sigma^2$表示数据点对应的均值方差。 和batch normalization一样，layer normalization最后也对数据做一个自适应的偏移和放大/缩小，使用一样也是$\gamma$和$\beta$两个参数。而且这两个参数是在同一个维度的数据上进行变换的，和batch normalization一样。 $$ y_{nd} = \gamma_d \hat{x_{nd}} + \beta_d $$

实验验证

经过layer normalization，每个数据点的均值都接近$\gamma$，方差都接近$\beta$。 </font>

LN层的反向传播

这里给的输入有上层的求导结果，也就是对LN层输出y的求导结果$\frac{dL}{dy}$。
cache中有
正向传播后传过来的LN层输入x
需要训练的参数$\beta$和$\gamma$
正向传播过程得到的batch数据每个数据点的的均值$\mu$和方差$\sigma^2$。
正向传播过程得到的归一化的数据$\hat{x}$
需要求的是三个导数：$\frac{dL}{dx}, \frac{dL}{d\gamma}, \frac{dL}{d\beta}$

对输入x的求导

根据链式法则有： $$ \frac{dL}{dx} = \frac{dL}{d\hat{x}}*\frac{d\hat{x}}{x} + \frac{dL}{d\sigma^2}*\frac{d\sigma^2}{x} + \frac{dL}{d\mu}*\frac{d\mu}{dx} $$ 接下来推导每一项的计算： $$ \begin{align} \frac{dL}{d\hat{x}} &= \frac{dL}{dy} * \frac{dy}{d\hat{x}} = \frac{dL}{dy} * \gamma\\ \frac{d\hat{x}}{dx} &= \frac{dL}{dy} * \frac{1}{\sqrt{\sigma^2_d+\epsilon}}\\ \frac{dL}{d\sigma^2} &= \sum{\frac{\frac{dL}{d\hat{x}} * [-(x-\mu)]}{2(\sigma^2+\epsilon)^{-\frac{3}{2}}}} \\ \frac{d\sigma^2}{x} &= \frac{2(x-\mu)}{m}\\ \frac{dL}{d\mu} &= (\sum \frac{dL}{d\hat{x}}\frac{-1}{\sqrt{\sigma^2_d+\epsilon}}) + \frac{dL}{d\sigma^2}\frac{\sum -2(x-\mu)}{m}\\ \frac{d\mu}{dx} &= \frac{1}{m} \end{align} $$ 上面的计算和batch normalization的过程是一样的。但是由于layer normalization的归一化是在数据点的基础之上的，于是在求和的维度选择上也有所不同。
于是$\frac{dL}{d\sigma^2}$ 的形状应该是$\sigma^2$的形状，也就是N维的向量，其中D为batch size，因为是对数据点进行的归一化，使用的方差就应该和数据点一样多个。等号右边的$\sum$后面却是形状为N×D的矩阵，其中D是数据维度，可见求和应该是沿着D的维度求和得到N维向量。
同样$\frac{dL}{d\mu}$也是N维的向量，第一个$\sum$后面也是N×D维的矩阵，应该沿着D维求和。第二个$\sum$后面的矩阵由于是由$x$计算出来，也应该是N×D的矩阵，求和结果其实是一个数据点各个维度的数值求和后减去均值的求和，这两个数是相等的，于是后面一项为0。

参数的求导 ($\gamma \& \beta$)

由$\hat{x}$计算得到$y$的过程和batch normalization是完全一致的，因此求导过程也是一样的。
$$ \frac{dL}{d\gamma} = \sum\frac{dL}{dy}*\hat{x}\\ \frac{dL}{d\beta} = \sum\frac{dL}{dy}\\ $$ $\gamma$和$\beta$都是D维的向量，于是求和也是沿着N维的方向进行求和。
